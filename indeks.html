Hydroponic Pak Choy (Sawi) Growth Optimization

In modern Malaysian agrotechnology (such as smart vertical farms in Cameron Highlands or automated greenhouse systems in Serdang), crop growth parameters are monitored using IoT sensors. Measuring biomass growth in terms of absolute fresh weight $f(x)$ allows agrotech managers to calculate the dynamic growth rate—mathematically defined as the first derivative $f'(x)$. By targeting nutritional delivery (Electrical Conductivity, EC) to match the peak instantaneous growth rate, farms can reduce fertilizer waste, cut carbon footprints, and maximize yield efficiency.

Source: MARDI Agro-Technology Journal (2024) SOP: Department of Agriculture (DOA) Malaysia MyGAP Standards

Smart Agrotech Parameters

  • Crop Cultivar: Pak Choy F1 Hybrid
  • Growth Medium: Recirculating Deep Water Culture (DWC)
  • Avg. Cycle: 40 Days (Nursery to Harvest)
  • Target Weight: 250g per plant

Cumulative Biomass Function

Logistic curve representing plant mass over time

$$f(x) = \frac{L}{1 + e^{-k(x - x_0)}}$$

\(f(x)\): Estimated dry/fresh weight (grams) at day \(x\).

\(L\): Maximum carrying capacity / final target biomass (grams).

\(k\): Growth steepness rate (affected by temperature/nutrient concentration).

\(x_0\): Dynamic inflection point (day of absolute fastest growth rate).

Instantaneous Growth Rate (Derivative)

Calculates growth rate change (g/day) at day \(x\)

$$f'(x) = \frac{L \cdot k \cdot e^{-k(x - x_0)}}{(1 + e^{-k(x - x_0)})^2}$$

\(f'(x)\): Biomass growth rate in grams per day.

Peak Rate: Always occurs at inflection day \(x_0\) where \(f''(x) = 0\).

Utility: Helps agrotechnologists identify maximum nitrogen absorption windows for fertigation.

Smart Agri-Fertigation Optimization Workflow

1

Nursery & Rooting Phase

Interval: Days 0 to 10
During this initial phase, the derivative value \(f'(x)\) is low (< 2 g/day). Photosynthesis is localized to early true leaves.

Agrotech Intervention: Keep EC range lower (1.2–1.5 mS/cm). High nitrogen will burn tender root zones.
2

Peak Vegetative Synthesis

Interval: Days 11 to 28
The growth rate \(f'(x)\) accelerates dramatically, peaking precisely at inflection Day \(x_0\). Leaves are actively compounding biomass.

Agrotech Intervention: Peak metabolism! Crank EC levels up to 2.0 mS/cm and keep automated aeration pumps operating continuously.
3

Pre-Harvest Flushing Stage

Interval: Days 29 to 40
The derivative \(f'(x)\) declines rapidly toward zero. Plant cell wall elongation is complete; biomass stabilizes at target carrying capacity \(L\).

Agrotech Intervention: Flush hydroponic reservoirs with pure reverse-osmosis water to drop nitrate levels below MyGAP safety boundaries.
